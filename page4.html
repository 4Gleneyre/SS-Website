What makes Bill Gates a great leader?

Adhering to visionary ideas and evolving continuously 

responsive site templates
“It’s fine to celebrate success, but it is more important to heed the lessons of failure.” 
- Bill Gates

- During the course of business development, Gates faced many obstacles such as the failure of his first company, Traf-O-Data.  However, he never gave up and continued his career to create Altair BASIC, which led to foundation of MS-DOS. 

- As the inventor of the first Microsoft Windows in 1985, Gates was able to recognize the growth potential to develop diverse Microsoft product lines.  His goal was for there to be "a computer on every desk and in every home."

“We all need people who will give us feedback. That’s how we improve.” - Bill Gates
Bill Gates constantly seeked advice from others and learned from mentors such as Warren Buffett.

- Gates had an optimistic attitude regardless of what happened as he continued to look forward, especially during the difficult time when the company was dealing with an anti-trust lawsuit.  

- Gates says he reads 50 books a year, stating “reading is still the main way that I both learn new things and test my understanding.” 

Summary: Bill Gates is not just a knowledgeable expert, but also a man with vision. It was the vision that inspired him to be a great leader and create a new OS platform. Meanwhile, Gates acknowledges failure is inevitable and he was able to learn from failure.
