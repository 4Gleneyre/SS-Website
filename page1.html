What makes Bill Gates a great leader?

Following his passions and working hard to pursue them

"Intellectual property has the shelf life of banana."
- Bill Gates

- At a young age, Bill Gates was always fascinated by science. He read the entire “World Book Encyclopedia” series from beginning to end.  

- Gates started programming at 13 years old.

- At Harvard, even though his major was pre-law, he overloaded himself with mathematical and computer science courses and spent a tremendous amount of time on the school’s computer.  

- Despite his parents' disappointment, Gates dropped out of Harvard after he and Paul Allen launched their own software company.

- Gates worked relentlessly not only the early years of Microsoft, but also after Microsoft gained success.  He never stopped what he loved the most - writing code.  He continued reviewing code and often rewrote code for Microsoft's products.  

"I never took a day off in my twenties. Not one. "
"Like my friend Warren Buffett, I feel particularly lucky to do something every day that I love to do. He calls it ‘tap-dancing to work."
- Bill Gates
Summary: As a leader, Bill Gates’ passion inspire others to pursue what one loves to do. Also, passion and robust action are necessary for a leadership to reach and sustain a success.
